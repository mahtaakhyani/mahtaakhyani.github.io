Social Robot for ASC Children

Social Robot for eliciting empathy to help children with Autism

  • 2022
  • By Mahta
  • All rights of this project is reserved for Advanced Robotic Laboratory at Department of Electrical and computer engineering, University of Tehran.
  • Other participants: Alireza Kargar(Solid body designer), Shahab Nikkhoo (First Creator)

Affective expressions are one of the effective communication channels among humans and many other animals, which can significantly affect quality of interactions. This communication approach has also attracted interest in robotics and virtual agent research, in particular when the robots or agents directly interact with humans. The capability to perceive and express emotions is considered to be important for creating truly socially interactive robots.
Many different types of robots have been used by various research groups to engage autistic children in playful interactions.
Findings from studies revealed that pre-school children with ASC showed increased levels of eye gaze (the duration the child gazed at the interaction partner’s face smile/laughter behaviours when interacting with the robot rather than when interacting with a human. In addition, Simut et al. reported that children with ASC showed increased eye contact with the social robot compared to the human partner during a play task.

What exactly is Autism?

Autism Spectrum Condition (ASC) is a developmental condition that is becoming increasingly prevalent, possibly due to increased awareness of the condition and changes in the diagnostic criteria. ASC is a spectrum of conditions that appears in many different forms and varies in its degrees of severity from severe low functioning autism, to high functioning and Asperger’s syndrome. A key characteristic of ASC is impairment of social communication and interaction . Children with ASC often find it difficult to engage in social or collaborative play activities with other children and can be quite solitary which can adversely affect their development.
Children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) are defined by their abnormal or impaired development in social interaction and communication, as well as restricted and repetitive behaviors, interests, or activities. The rapid growth of ASD in the past 20 years has inspired many research efforts toward the diagnosis and rehabilitation of ASD.
