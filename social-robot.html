A social robot to interact with children on the autism spectrum

Written by Mahta
.
This project was initially built by a group of graduate students in favor of representing a robotic platform to help diagnose and/or increase cognitive abilities in children with autism.
After I contributed to this project as a back-end developer, ROS developer, and mechanical engineer (almost an intern) and somehow it is safe to say, I didn't have a specific role defined but to extend and complete this platform: As I did.

A summary of my contribution

Personal Experience


My Experience from this period of my professional life was mostly formed by getting to know what the challanges of building something from scratch might be. Honestly, I was mostly overwhelmed by this huge mist of "unknowns" around each and every step of the way, which I was trying to lead my team through. As the person who felt the responsibilty to be the one who stays put along the path, it was such a burdon not being sure about any descision all along.

On the other hand, having the chance to be a part of the team with many enthusiastic, skilled, and active individuals was - and is - one of the best pros of starting an at least tried to be-startup. I learned a lot of things about planning a business, digital marketing, entrepernureship and so on from my teammates.

Aditionally, watching my friends rise and flourish by finding out their true passions, or some activity they didn't know they would enjoy, still warms my heart. I'm truly happy that I have had even a small positive impact on their lives.

History

Fanjoo was founded in April 2020 (During COVID-19 lockdown) by Mahta Akhyani and Mr. Parsa Mashayekh through a telegram channel, and then nourished by 5 other talented students. 

We managed to have our weekly meetings on Skype, giving updates on what we have done during the week and what are we up to next. I was in charge of managing the scheduals for assigned tasks and extra meetings. Also, after 3 months, I had one-on-one meetings with each of my teammates each day to evaluate the mental state of each individual towards Fanjoo, what we have done, and most importantly, each person's goals and future view and expectations from this startup as a bussiness; All for getting to know them better and help making a more harmonic interaction in the team to increase performance and satisfaction during our time together as colleagues.

After a while we splited up to a tech team leaded by me and a social networks-marketing team with Sara in charge. Our group was mostly focused on developing the web platform ( HTML-CSS-JS + Django REST) while all 6 of us engaging in weekly brain stormings to mature the idea concerning the new circumstances.

Unfortunately, Fanjoo was called off in Jan 2021, due to some resource limitations.
